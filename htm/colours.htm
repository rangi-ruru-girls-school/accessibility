Colours

Colour blindness affects 1 in 12 men (8%) and 1 in 200 women (0.5%). The most common type is red/green colour blindness which makes it difficult for people to see the difference between red and green.

Sites like Coolors.co and Adobe Color usually take into account colour blindness when they generate a colour palette. In Coolors, you can choose specific types of colour blindness to generate palettes for.

Some things you can do to make your HTML more semantic:

  • Avoid red and green elements near one another.
  • Avoid red text on a green background, and vice versa.
  • Review your site through a tool like Chromatic Vision Simulator for iOS and Android.
